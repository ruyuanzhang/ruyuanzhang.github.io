Computational Cognition and Artificial Intelligence

This course serves as an elective course for psychology PhD and master students who have interests in computational cognition, computational neuroscience, and brain-like artificial intelligence. Students are expected to (1) have basic understandings of the principles of computational cognition; (2) understand the major theories and techniques of computational modeling in cognitive neuroscience; (3) obtain an overview of the relationships between modern artificial intelligence and psychology. This course can be divided into three parts. The first part focuses on basic knowledge of Bayesian inference in cognitive modeling. The second part introduces classical computational models in various topics in cognitive science, including perceptual decision-making, visual working memory, and reinforcement learning. The third part covers the basics of deep neural networks and their relations to human information processing.
